Weather App using React Native

During my time at KTH I had a module called developing mobile apps. The premise of this course was to learn about how to make apps while learning about the distinction between a good app that people will enjoy and an app that poeple will soon get fed up with and wont use. Some of the main take aways from the course, besides a basic understanding on how to create a mobile app, was that apps need to be simple, require as little effort as possible and convey information in a clear and concise manner.

The idea that I had for the module was to make a weather app aimed at poeple who use the weather for sport and recreation. The module was more about the making, testing and developing of an app rather than having a groundbreaking idea.




Above is the finished app. Although there is nothing revolutionary (I have purposely left out some of the earlier renderings) about it I learned a lot about what is involved in making apps. I learning about how you can take an idea, build it, get user feedback and adapt it to make something that the target market would actually use. I feel a lot more confident now about the world of app development.
